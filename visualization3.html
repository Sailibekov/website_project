Analysis of the Relationships Among Academic Factors

Correlation Matrix - Altair

Key Insights

  • Overall Relationships:

    Our correlation matrix reveals several insights on the factors that influence student performance by showing which variables have the most meaningful relationships with exam outcomes.

  • Attendance:

    Attendance shows the strongest positive correlation with exam scores (0.58), indicating that students who attend class more regularly tend to perform better on exams.

  • Study Hours:

    Hours studied shows the second strongest relationship (0.45), suggesting that consistent studying contributes to higher exam outcomes.

  • Previous Scores & Tutoring:

    Previous scores and the number of tutoring sessions show smaller but still positive correlations (0.18 and 0.16, respectively), implying that past performance and academic support play roles in student success.

  • Physical Activity & Sleep:

    Surprisingly, factors such as physical activity (0.03) and sleep hours (–0.02) show little to no correlation with exam scores, suggesting that these factors may have minimal direct impact on academic performance.
