Exploring Attendance Trends and Their Impact on Performance

Key Insights on Attendance and Exam Scores

  • Higher Attendance, Higher Scores: Students with 91–100% attendance have significantly higher median exam scores compared to those with 60–70% attendance, highlighting a strong positive correlation between regular class attendance and exam performance.
  • Greater Variability in High Attendance: Although high attendance generally leads to better outcomes, the exam scores in this group show a wider spread, suggesting that additional factors may influence performance among regular attendees.
  • Consistent Low Scores for Low Attendance: The low-attendance group exhibits a tighter clustering of lower exam scores, underscoring the negative impact of irregular attendance on academic success.
